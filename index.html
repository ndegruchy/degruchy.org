Things I Don't Need # ^

2020-09-01 11:01:04

So, I put this little section together for a couple of reasons:

  1. I still have a lot of things that don't fit within the context of a "toot" of ~500 chars.
  2. I like messing with my website, HTML and CSS
  3. I feel like it

Those fine reasons notwithstanding, I wanted to go over the idea of things I don't want or need. As I get older, I find that the more stuff I have to keep running, the less interested I am.

That means if you have more than two components that need to be setup for things to work (App+DB+OtherCrap), or you have a complicated setup like Docker, virutal envrionments or otherwise require a lot of hand-holding or babysitting to get working -- yeah, sorry, I don't care.

That weeds out a lot of applications. Even popular and otherwise "just fine" applications that I like. However, in the spirit of simplicity, I'm willing to put up with an astonishing amount of "extra" work. Things like using SSH to ferry files around instead of setting up a file share or webapp, using local news readers, like newsboat, to read websites. These things are simple and to the point. I generally just set them up and get to going.

Turns out you don't need a lot of the stuff you have. Some of it's nice, some of it's super convienient, but entirely unnecessary. Try it out sometime. You may find that it frees you from having to deal with stuff you didn't even want.
